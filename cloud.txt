Cloud Computing Overview

Clouds are collections of water droplets or ice crystals suspended in the atmosphere. They play a crucial role in Earth's weather and climate systems.

Cloud Formation:
Water evaporates from oceans, lakes, and rivers, rising into the atmosphere. As air cools, water vapor condenses around tiny particles to form clouds. This process is essential for the water cycle, distributing moisture and energy across the planet.

Climate Impact:
Clouds reflect sunlight back to space and trap heat from the Earth's surface, regulating global temperature. They are vital for weather patterns, precipitation, and sustaining life on Earth.
